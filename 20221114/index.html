About Okinawa

Okinawa was once an indepent country which was ruled by the Ryukyu kindomg, and flourished through trade with China - the largest country in asia - as well as other neighboring countries. After the invasion of Satsuma in 1609, Ryukyu became part of Japan's shogunate system. It became a prefecture of Japan due to the Abolition of the Han System and Establishment of the Prefecture systems. which occurred in 1879.

During the Pacific War, Okinawa was the site of the only land battle in Japan that involved civilians, After the war, Okinawa was placed under the admininstation of the United States. In 1972 however, Okinawa was returned to Japanese admininstation. Okinawa remains under Japanese admininstation today.

Famous Foods

Okinawa soba is a type of noodle produced in Okinawa Prefecture, Japan. Okinawa soba is a regional collective trademark of the Okinawan Noodle Manufactureing Co-op. On Okinawa, it is sometimes simply called soba, although this Japanese term typically refers to buckwheat noodles in mainland Japan.
